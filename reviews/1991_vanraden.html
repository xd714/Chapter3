Reliability Framework & Daughter Equivalents
WHY - Daughter Equivalents Concept
Intuitive Information Measure: Daughter equivalents provide easily interpreted units for measuring evaluation accuracy.
  • Standard Daughter: One record, infinite management group mates, perfect mate REL
  • Additive Information: DE from parents, self, and progeny sum to total DE
  • Traditional Connection: Relates to historical sire evaluation accuracy measures
  • Cross-Sex Comparison: Enables comparison of bull and cow evaluations
  • Simple Interpretation: More DE always means higher reliability
Advantage over Record Equivalents: Only one sex has records; DE apply to both sexes.
HOW - Reliability Calculation
Step-by-Step Process: Systematic approach to calculating evaluation accuracy from information sources.
  • DE‚Çö‚Çê Calculation: Function of parent REL excluding animal's contribution

  • DE‚Çö·µ£‚Çíg-mate Calculation: Progeny contribution adjusted for mate REL and herd-sire interaction
  • Total DE: Sum of all daughter equivalent sources
  • Final REL: REL = DE/(DE + 14) for USDA system
Iterative Strategy: Process animals by age to ensure parent REL available before calculating progeny contributions.
WHAT - Information Sources Quantified
Typical DE Contributions: Examples showing relative importance of different information pathways.
