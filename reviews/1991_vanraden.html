Derivation, Calculation, and Use of National Animal Model Information

Comprehensive Framework for Genetic Evaluation Methodology

Authors: P.M. VanRaden, G.R. Wiggans

Institution: Animal Improvement Programs Laboratory, Agricultural Research Service, USDA

View Original Paper

Paper Information & Historical Context

Citation: VanRaden, P.M., & Wiggans, G.R. (1991). Derivation, Calculation, and Use of National Animal Model Information. Journal of Dairy Science, 74:2737-2746

Historical Significance: This paper provided the foundational mathematical framework for USDA genetic evaluations using animal models, introducing key terminology and calculation methods still used today.

Key Innovations:

  • Systematic derivation of Predicted Transmitting Ability (PTA) components
  • Introduction of Daughter Equivalent (DE) concept for measuring information
  • Mathematical framework for Yield Deviation (YD) and Daughter Yield Deviation (DYD)
  • Reliability calculation methodology for animal model evaluations
Mathematical Model Framework
1
Basic Animal Model Structure
The foundational animal model equation incorporating all genetic and environmental effects
y = Mm + Za + ZAₘg + Pp + Cc + e
Components:
• y = standardized yield (milk, fat, protein)
• m = management group effects
• a = additive genetic merit
• g = unknown-parent group effects
• p = permanent environmental effects
• c = herd-sire interaction effects
• e = random error
2
Yield Deviation Calculation
Extract animal's genetic information from performance records
YD = yi - (mi + pi + ci)
Purpose: Summarize cow's own performance adjusted for non-genetic effects
Properties: Weighted average of standardized yields minus solutions for management, permanent environment, and herd-sire effects
Use: Primary input for cow's genetic evaluation
3
PTA Decomposition
Break down Predicted Transmitting Ability into component sources
PTA = w1PA + w2(YD/2) + w3PC
Three Information Sources:
• PA = Parent Average (genetic information from ancestors)
• YD/2 = Half of Yield Deviation (own performance)
• PC = Progeny Contribution (information from descendants)
Weights: w₁ + w₂ + w₃ = 1, derived from mixed model equations
4
Daughter Yield Deviation (DYD)
Unregressed measure of progeny performance for bulls
DYD = Σqₚᵣₒₘw₂ₚᵣₒₘ(YDₚᵣₒₘ - PTAₘₐₜₑ)/Σqₚᵣₒₘw₂ₚᵣₒₘ
Advantage: More independent measure than Progeny Contribution
Application: Reported for bulls with 10+ daughters
Properties: Not influenced by bull's own PTA, excludes grandprogeny information
Reliability and Daughter Equivalents Framework
Reliability Definition
REL = r²(PTA, TA)

Squared correlation between predicted and true transmitting ability

REL = DE/(DE + 14)

Practical calculation using Daughter Equivalents

Daughter Equivalent Sources
DE_total = DE_PA + DE_yield + DE_progeny

Total information from three sources:

  • Parents (DE_PA)
  • Own records (DE_yield)
  • Progeny adjusted for mates (DE_progeny)
5
Information Measurement in Daughter Equivalents
Standardized units for measuring genetic information contribution
Standard Daughter Definition:
• One lactation record
• Infinite management group mates
• Other parent with perfect reliability

Advantages over Record Equivalents:
• Simpler interpretation
• Traditional connection to sire evaluation
• Accounts for both sexes contributing information
Practical Examples and Implementation
Information Source Example Scenario Daughter Equivalents Reliability Contribution
Parents Sire 99% REL, Dam 50% REL 8.3 High
Own Performance 3 lactation records 7.8 Moderate-High
Progeny 10 daughters, 10 herds 1.8 Low-Moderate
Progeny 100 daughters, 100 herds 5.4 Moderate
6
Herd-Sire Interaction Effects
Adjustment for daughters in the same herd
Problem: Daughters in same herd provide less independent information
Solution: Reduced DE contribution per daughter
Formula: DE_progeny-mate = 1/(0.16 + 0.84/d)
where d = number of daughters in herd

Example: 10 daughters in one herd contribute 4.1 DE total (0.41 per daughter) vs. 10 daughters in separate herds contributing 1.8 DE total (0.18 per daughter)
Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Problem Statement
Industry Need: Transition from sire models to comprehensive animal models required new terminology and calculation methods.
  • Previous MCC system inadequate for complex relationships
  • Need for standardized accuracy measures
  • Industry required interpretable genetic evaluations
  • Mathematical foundations needed documentation
Technical Challenge: Derive practical formulas from complex mixed model equations while maintaining statistical rigor.
HOW - Methodology
Mathematical Approach: Systematic derivation from mixed model equations to practical formulas.
  • Model Definition: Establish animal model with all relevant effects
  • Component Derivation: Extract YD, PA, PC from equation solutions
  • Information Theory: Develop DE framework for accuracy measurement
  • Practical Implementation: Create computational algorithms for large-scale evaluation
Validation: Mathematical proofs linking to established selection index theory.
WHAT - Key Contributions
Theoretical Advances: Comprehensive mathematical framework for animal model information.
  • PTA decomposition into interpretable components
  • Daughter Equivalent standardization of information units
  • DYD methodology for unregressed progeny evaluation
  • Reliability formulas accounting for all information sources
Practical Impact: Became the foundation for USDA genetic evaluations and influenced international breeding programs.
Legacy and Modern Relevance

Enduring Impact: This paper established the mathematical and conceptual framework that continues to underpin modern genetic evaluation systems. The terminology (PTA, DE, REL, DYD) and calculation methods introduced here remain standard in dairy cattle breeding worldwide.

Modern Applications:

  • Genomic evaluations build upon this animal model foundation
  • Daughter Equivalent concepts adapted for genomic information measurement
  • International genetic evaluation systems use these methodologies
  • Reliability calculations remain fundamental to breeding decisions

Educational Value: Provides clear mathematical derivations connecting complex linear algebra to practical breeding applications, making it an essential reference for quantitative genetics education.
