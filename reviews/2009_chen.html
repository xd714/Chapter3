Detailed Material and Methods Flow
1
Population Simulation Setup
Create founder generation and simulate population evolution over time
Initial: 500 males + 500 females (total 1000)
Process: Random mating for 1000 generations
Population size: Constant at 1000 individuals
2
Bottleneck Simulation
Simulate genetic bottleneck to mimic real breeding scenarios
Generation 1001: Start bottleneck
Generations 1001-1010: Progressive reduction to 100 individuals
Purpose: Mimic intensive selection pressure
3
Population Recovery
Expand population and establish final breeding structure
Generation 1011: Increase to 500 individuals
Generations 1011-1025: Random mating at 500 per generation
Final structure: 100 groups of 5 full-siblings each
4
Genome Architecture
Define genetic structure and mutation parameters
Chromosomes: 10 total

5
Trait and Phenotype Generation
Create breeding values and observable phenotypes

Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Problem Statement
Main Issue: Traditional breeding methods increase inbreeding while reducing genetic diversity.
  • BLUP accelerates genetic gain but also inbreeding rate
  • Few elite sires used intensively (especially Holstein)
  • Risk of losing genetic variation
  • Cannot predict Mendelian sampling terms without progeny data
Opportunity: Use abundant SNP data to predict the unpredictable genetic component.
HOW - Methodology
Key Innovation: Two-step genomic approach to predict Mendelian sampling terms.
  • Step 1: Use conventional model to estimate breeding values and derive Mendelian sampling terms
  • Step 2: Use SNP markers to predict genomic Mendelian sampling effects
  • Combine: Parent average + genomic Mendelian sampling prediction
Validation: Compare against conventional method across multiple heritability and selection scenarios.
WHAT - Key Findings
Results: Genomic model outperforms conventional approach.
  • Lower relationships among selected animals
  • More family groups represented in selection
  • Higher prediction accuracy (0.583 vs 0.533 at h¬≤=0.5)
  • Benefits increase with higher heritability
Impact: Enables sustainable breeding with maintained diversity and improved accuracy.
