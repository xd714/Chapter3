Computational Framework Development
1
Enhanced MSV Computation
Develop computationally efficient representation of Mendelian Sampling Variance prediction
Innovation: Parent-specific marker effects vector mi
Speed improvement: 73-fold faster than previous methods
Formula:
var(bi) = mi'Rmi
Key advantage: Single universal matrix R for all parents
2
Haplotype Similarity Measure
Define similarity between parents based on shared segregation patterns
Core concept: Quantify shared heterozygous markers with large effects
Similarity formula:
si,j = |mi'Rmj|
Interpretation: High similarity = many shared chromosome segments
Self-similarity: Equals individual's MSV

Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Critical Challenge
Core Problem: Genomic selection accelerates genetic gain but rapidly depletes genetic diversity.
  • Traditional methods focus on short-term gains
  • Existing approaches lack trait-specific diversity control
  • OCS controls inbreeding but not haplotype diversity
  • Risk of losing valuable genetic variants for future adaptation
Opportunity: Develop similarity matrix that captures haplotype relationships for sustainable breeding decisions.
HOW - Methodological Innovation
Key Breakthrough: Similarity matrix based on Mendelian sampling patterns.
  • Step 1: Efficient MSV computation using parent-specific marker vectors
  • Step 2: Quantify pairwise haplotype similarities via bilinear forms
  • Step 3: Construct similarity matrix S with MSVs on diagonal
  • Step 4: Apply MOCS optimization with haplotype diversity constraints
Validation: Test on Holstein data and 50-generation simulations across multiple breeding scenarios.
WHAT - Key Achievements
Results: MOCS with similarity matrix outperforms traditional methods.
  • 73-fold speed improvement in MSV computation
  • Up to 4.5 standard deviations more long-term genetic variability
  • 5-44% more favorable QTL alleles retained
  • Reduced inbreeding while maintaining genetic gain
Impact: Enables sustainable genomic selection with preserved haplotype diversity for long-term breeding success.
