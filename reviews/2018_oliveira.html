Paper Overview & Research Context

Core Challenge: In dairy cattle genomic evaluation, when raw phenotypic data aren't available, estimated breeding values (EBVs) must be used as pseudophenotypes. However, using EBVs directly can cause double-counting of information and bias in genomic predictions.

Research Question: Which deregression method performs best for converting EBVs to unbiased pseudophenotypes in genomic prediction of test-day dairy traits?

Innovation: First comprehensive comparison of deregression methods specifically for longitudinal (test-day) traits in dairy cattle, examining three scenarios based on reliability thresholds.

Deregression Methods Comparison
VanRaden (VR) Method
Approach: Standard deregression formula
dEBV_ij = PA_ij + (EBV_ij - PA_ij) / R_i
Key Features:
‚Ä¢ Uses reliability-based deregression factor
‚Ä¢ Separates parent average from own performance
‚Ä¢ Most widely used method

Performance:
‚Ä¢ Good reliability and low bias
‚Ä¢ Stable across scenarios
‚Ä¢ No weighting required
Wiggans (WG) Method
Approach: VR method + cow adjustment
Adjust cow dEBVs to match bull distribution
Key Features:
‚Ä¢ Variance adjustment (VA) by reliability groups
‚Ä¢ Mean adjustment (MA) for each group
‚Ä¢ Addresses cow-bull bias differences

Performance:
‚Ä¢ Similar reliability to VR
‚Ä¢ Reduces cow-bull differences
‚Ä¢ Effective for preferential treatment bias
Garrick (GR) Method
Approach: Mixed model equations approximation
Solves system: W'_PA W_PA + Œªk vs W'_IndPA W_IndPA + 2k
Key Features:
‚Ä¢ More complex mathematical formulation
‚Ä¢ Accounts for information content directly
‚Ä¢ Theoretically sophisticated

Performance:
‚Ä¢ Lower reliability than VR/WG
‚Ä¢ Too strong deregression
‚Ä¢ Higher variance in dEBVs
Experimental Design & Methodology
1
Data Source & Population Structure
Canadian Jersey cattle with official genetic evaluations for test-day traits
Training Population: Animals born 1960-2010 (from 2012 evaluation)
Validation Population: Animals born 2011-2014 (from 2016 evaluation)
Traits: Milk yield (MY), fat yield (FY), protein yield (PY), somatic cell score (SCS)
Random Regression: 4th-order Legendre polynomials for lactation curves
2
Reliability-Based Scenarios
Three scenarios based on minimum reliability thresholds (REL_EBV)
SCEN1: REL_EBV > average parent reliability in both populations
SCEN2: REL_EBV > 0.50 in training, > average parent reliability in validation
SCEN3: REL_EBV > 0.50 in both training and validation
Purpose: Test effect of reliability thresholds on prediction accuracy
3
Genomic Prediction Implementation
GBLUP with random regression model using deregressed EBVs as pseudophenotypes
Model: y = Xb + Zg + e (4th-order Legendre polynomials)
Weighting: Unweighted vs. weighted by effective record contributions
Genotypes: 38,166 SNPs after quality control
Software: AIREMLF90 for variance components and solutions
4
Evaluation Metrics
Comprehensive assessment of prediction quality and bias
Reliability: r¬≤_dEBV, r¬≤_DGV, adjusted r¬≤_DGV
Bias: Regression coefficient of dEBV on DGV (b_DGV)
Separate Analysis: Bulls vs. cows performance
Total Analyses: 18 per trait (3 methods √ó 3 scenarios √ó 2 weighting)

Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Biological & Statistical Challenge
Deregression Necessity:
‚Ä¢ EBVs contain parent average information
‚Ä¢ Using raw EBVs causes double-counting
‚Ä¢ Double shrinkage reduces prediction accuracy

Test-Day Complexity:
‚Ä¢ Longitudinal traits require special handling
‚Ä¢ Lactation curve shape must be preserved
‚Ä¢ Bulls vs. cows have different evaluation bias
HOW - Methodological Approach
Deregression Process:
‚Ä¢ Remove parent average contribution from EBV
‚Ä¢ Weight by reliability to avoid over-correction
‚Ä¢ WG method adjusts cow bias

Validation Strategy:
‚Ä¢ Time-based split (2012 vs. 2016 evaluations)
‚Ä¢ Multiple reliability thresholds tested
‚Ä¢ Separate bull/cow analysis reveals bias patterns
WHAT - Practical Recommendations
Best Practice:
‚Ä¢ Use VR or WG methods
‚Ä¢ SCEN2 reliability threshold optimal
‚Ä¢ Unweighted analysis generally sufficient

Implementation:
‚Ä¢ Preserve lactation curve coefficients
‚Ä¢ Consider cow preferential treatment
‚Ä¢ Monitor bull vs. cow prediction bias
Technical Implementation Details
A
Cow Bias Adjustment (WG Method)
Addresses preferential treatment of genotyped cows in Jersey population:
PA_adj = PA_sire + [VA √ó (PA_dam - MA)]
dEBV_adj,cows = VA √ó (dEBV - MA) + PA_adj
This adjustment makes cow evaluations comparable to bull evaluations.
B
Reliability-Based Population Selection
Strategic thresholds balance accuracy and population size:
Average REL_PA: 0.35 (MY), 0.35 (FY), 0.34 (PY), 0.31 (SCS)
Population Impact: SCEN2/3 had 20-55% fewer animals than SCEN1
Cow Proportion: ~40% except SCS (17% in SCEN2/3)
Implications for Dairy Breeding

Immediate Impact: Provides clear guidance for implementing genomic prediction in dairy breeding programs using test-day evaluations, particularly important for breeds with smaller populations that include cow records.

Methodological Significance: Demonstrates that established deregression methods developed for accumulated traits work well for longitudinal traits, with VR and WG methods providing nearly equivalent performance.

Future Applications: The finding that weighting is generally unnecessary simplifies implementation in breeding programs, while the cow adjustment method (WG) provides a solution for addressing preferential treatment bias in female evaluations.
