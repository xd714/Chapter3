Evolutionary Origin of Genomic Structural Variations in Domestic Yaks

Comprehensive Analysis of Graph-Genome and Pangenomic Variations

Authors: Liu, X., Liu, W., Lenstra, J.A., Zheng, Z., Wu, X., Yang, J., Li, B., Yang, Y., Qiu, Q., Liu, H., Li, K., Liang, C., Guo, X., Ma, X., Abbott, R.J., Kang, M., Yan, P., Liu, J.

Journal: Nature Communications (2023)

Institution: Lanzhou University, Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences, Utrecht University

View Original Paper

Research Overview

Citation: Liu, X., Liu, W., Lenstra, J.A. et al. Evolutionary origin of genomic structural variations in domestic yaks. Nat Commun 14, 5617 (2023).

Key Innovation: This study represents the first comprehensive graph-genome analysis of structural variants (SVs) across bovine species, distinguishing between natural selection, domestication, and introgression origins of genetic variants in domestic yaks.

Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Research Motivation
Critical Gap: Previous genomic studies of yaks were limited by low-quality assemblies and inability to distinguish evolutionary origins of genetic variants.
  • Yaks have undergone natural selection (high-altitude adaptation)
  • Subject to artificial selection during domestication
  • Experienced introgression from cattle
  • Structural variants (SVs) were poorly characterized despite their importance
Opportunity: Graph-genome approaches can comprehensively identify and trace origins of structural variations.
HOW - Methodology
Graph-Genome Approach: Multi-assembly graph construction with comprehensive variant calling.
  • Dataset: 47 high-quality genomes (28 new assemblies)
  • Species: 7 cross-fertile bovine species
  • Validation: 386 individuals with resequencing data
  • SV Detection: Bubble-popping algorithm identified 57,432 high-resolution SVs
  • Origin Tracing: Phylogenetic analysis of haplotypes
Innovation: First to systematically distinguish SV origins through comparative genomics.
WHAT - Key Discoveries
Major Findings: SVs have diverse evolutionary origins with functional consequences.
  • 334 genes with SVs under selection from wild yaks
  • 686 genes introgressed from cattle
  • Nearly 90% of domestic yaks show cattle introgression
  • White yak origin: KIT gene introgression from cattle
  • SVs significantly associated with gene expression changes
Impact: Provides framework for understanding domestication genetics and breeding implications.
Detailed Methodology Flow
1
Genome Assembly and Dataset Preparation
Comprehensive genome collection and high-quality assembly generation
New Assemblies: 28 genomes (6 wild yaks, 15 domestic yaks, 7 cattle)
Technology: PacBio HiFi and Oxford Nanopore
Total Dataset: 47 bovine genomes across 7 species
Quality: Average 24,368 protein-coding genes, 97.4% BUSCO completeness
2
Pangenome Construction
Build comprehensive gene catalogs for comparative analysis
Yak Pangenome: 50.18% core, 10.91% near-core, 38.91% variable genes
Saturation: Gene set approached saturation at n=20
Introgression: 119 genes in domestic yaks not in wild (56 from cattle)
Super-pangenome: All 7 bovine species integrated
3
Graph-Genome Construction
Multi-assembly graph for comprehensive SV detection
Graph Size: 3.14 Gb across 5,449,222 nodes
Non-reference: 387.0 Mb spanning novel sequences
Node Distribution: 60.8% core, 17.0% near-core, 22.2% variable
Algorithm: Minigraph with bubble-popping for SV calling
4
Structural Variant Detection and Genotyping
High-resolution SV calling across large sample set
Initial SVs: 293,712 detected (≥50 bp)
High-quality: 57,432 retained after filtering
Validation Set: 386 individuals (233 yaks, 140 cattle, others)
Size Distribution: 81.7% <500 bp, 99.76% <10 kb
5
Evolutionary Origin Classification
Phylogenetic analysis to trace SV origins and evolutionary history
Method: Neighbor-joining trees of 50-kb haplotype segments
Categories: Natural selection, domestication, introgression
Introgression Detection: 26,591 SVs (46.3%) involved in yak-cattle exchange
Geographic Pattern: Introgression varies by longitude (east to west decline)
6
Functional Validation and Expression Analysis
Experimental validation of SV functional impacts
Luciferase Assays: EPAS1, MB, DISC1, VWC2, SNX3 tested
Expression Analysis: RNA-seq from 178 transcriptomes
Hi-C Analysis: 3D genome organization changes
Validation: Significant association between SVs and gene expression
Key Biological Discoveries
High-Altitude Adaptation SVs
EPAS1 (HIF2α): 254 bp LINE1 insertion in wild yaks
  • Decreases promoter strength via DNA methylation
  • Associated with H733Y amino acid variant
  • Increases hypoxia response transcriptional activity
MB (Myoglobin): 155 bp deletion enhances expression
  • Higher enhancer activity in wild/domestic yaks
  • Increases oxygen storage capacity
  • Contains HIF-1α and EP300 binding sites
Domestication-Related SVs
DISC1: 277 bp insertion in domestic yaks
  • Increases gene expression levels
  • Associated with mental disorder pathways
  • May influence behavioral domestication traits
Neural Development Genes:
  • VWC2: 1070 bp deletion reduces neurogenesis
  • SNX3: 88 bp deletion affects neural tube closure
  • Consistent with craniofacial size reduction
White Yak Origin Discovery

Serial translocations of the KIT gene from cattle created white coat color variants. The study revealed that introgression of cattle Cs6 allele to chromosome 6, followed by yak-specific rearrangements creating multiple copies, resulted in KIT gene suppression and white coloration. Hi-C analysis showed this creates a closed chromatin compartment, reducing KIT expression in hair follicles.

Technical Innovations
Graph-Genome Approach
Advantages over Linear Reference:
  • Captures multi-allelic variants (40.8% of SVs)
  • Represents population-level diversity
  • Enables accurate SV genotyping
  • Reduces reference bias
Technical Implementation:
  • Minigraph for graph construction
  • Vg toolkit for variant genotyping
  • Custom PanPop pipeline for processing
Phylogenetic Origin Tracing
Methodology:
  • 50-kb haplotype segments flanking SVs
  • SNP-based phylogenetic trees
  • Species tree comparison for introgression
  • Geographic introgression mapping
Novel Insights:
  • Bidirectional introgression patterns
  • Geographic gradients of gene flow
  • Time-stratified introgression events

Population Genomics Insights
Introgression Patterns:

  • Cattle introgression decreases west to east
  • 91.5% of introgressed SVs overlap with transposable elements
  • Bidirectional gene flow with functional consequences

  • Disease resistance from cattle introgression
  • Morphological changes in domestication
  • Behavioral modifications through neural genes
Significance and Future Directions
Research Impact
This study establishes the first comprehensive framework for understanding how structural variants contribute to adaptation, domestication, and introgression in livestock species. The graph-genome approach provides a template for similar analyses in other species complexes.
Breeding Applications
The identification of functional SVs provides new targets for genomic selection in yak breeding. Understanding introgression patterns helps maintain genetic diversity while improving economically important traits. The database provides a resource for sustainable yak breeding programs.
Future Research Directions
  • Expanded Sampling: Include more geographic populations and related species
  • Functional Studies: Detailed characterization of candidate SVs
  • Breeding Applications: Implement SV-based genomic selection
  • Conservation: Maintain genetic diversity while improving traits
  • Comparative Studies: Apply framework to other livestock species
