Overview & Historical Significance
The Dummerstorf Selection Experiment: Started in 1969 at the Research Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN), this represents the world's longest continuous selection experiment in mice, spanning over 140 generations.

Unique Features:

  • Single founder population split into multiple trait-selected lines
  • Maintained unselected control line throughout entire experiment
  • Extreme phenotypes achieved: 2× litter size, 3× body mass, 3× endurance
  • First comprehensive genomic analysis of this historic experiment

Methodology & Genomic Analysis
Step 1: Sample Collection & Sequencing

Sampling Design: 150 mice total (25 females per line) from two time points

  • Time point 1: 10 females/line, 30× coverage (high coverage)
  • Time point 2: 15 females/line, 5× coverage (validation)
  • Strategy: Capture line-specific genetic variation while minimizing kinship
Step 2: Comprehensive Variant Discovery

Variant Calling: GATK best practices pipeline

  • SNPs: 5,099,945 single nucleotide polymorphisms
  • INDELs: 766,655 insertions/deletions
  • Structural Variants: Multiple SV callers (Manta, Whamg, Lumpy)
  • Annotation: SnpEff and VEP for functional impact prediction
Step 3: Population Genetic Analysis

Multi-level Analysis Approach:

  • Population Structure: Hierarchical clustering, admixture analysis
  • Genetic Diversity: Nucleotide diversity, runs of homozygosity (RoH)
  • Differentiation: FST-based sliding window analysis
  • Linkage Disequilibrium: Decay patterns across lines
Step 4: Trait-Specific Region Detection

Regions of Distinct Differentiation (RDDs):

  • Top 5% FST windows for target trait vs. control
  • Bottom 10% FST for all other comparisons
  • Conservative approach to minimize false positives
  • Gene annotation and functional enrichment analysis
Major Findings & Results
Dramatic Genetic Architecture Changes:
  • Genetic Diversity Loss: Trait-selected lines show 57.5-81.5% fixed loci vs. <25% in control
  • High Inbreeding: 65-78% of genome in runs of homozygosity
  • Strong Differentiation: FST values 0.44-0.61 between selected lines
  • Linkage Disequilibrium: Extensive LD in selected lines vs. rapid decay in control
Trait-Specific Candidate Genes

Fertility Lines (DUK/DUC):

  • DUK: Phospholipase D signaling, ovarian development (Tsc1, Trp63), placental vascularization (Atoh8)
  • DUC: Steroid hormone signaling, progesterone receptor with missense mutation (Pgr)
  • Shared: Olfactory receptors affecting fertilization, reproductive metabolism genes

Body Mass Lines (DU6/DU6P):

  • DU6: Energy metabolism (Hcrt), feed efficiency (Wdr27), bone formation (Smad5)
  • DU6P: Growth QTLs (Plag1, Chchd7), myotube formation (Piezo1), lipid content control (Cdh13)

Endurance Line (DUhLB):

  • Metabolism: Brown adipocyte development (Srsf6), lipid mobilization (Aldh3a2)
  • Exercise Response: Mitochondrial function (Il15r), cardiac adaptation (Atp5j)
Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Scientific Motivation
Unique Opportunity: First genomic analysis of the world's longest selection experiment.
  • Historical Value: 50+ years of continuous selection and maintenance
  • Extreme Phenotypes: Multiple traits pushed to biological limits
  • Controlled Design: Single founder population, maintained control line
  • Polygenic Complexity: Understanding genetic architecture of complex traits
Research Gap: Previous selection experiments lacked comprehensive genomic characterization of such extreme, long-term selected lines.
HOW - Methodological Innovation
Comprehensive Genomic Approach: Multi-scale analysis from SNPs to structural variants.
  • Whole Genome Sequencing: 150 mice across 6 lines, dual coverage strategy
  • Population Genomics: FST-based differentiation mapping
  • Structural Variation: Multiple SV callers for comprehensive detection
  • Conservative Statistics: Empirical thresholds to minimize false discoveries
Key Innovation: Regions of Distinct Differentiation (RDDs) - method to identify trait-specific genomic regions while controlling for drift.
WHAT - Key Discoveries
Genomic Consequences of Selection:
  • Massive diversity loss: Most beneficial alleles reached fixation
  • Trait-specific pathways: Different genetic routes to similar phenotypes
  • Structural variation role: Large-scale genomic rearrangements contribute to traits
  • Polygenic complexity: Multiple genes/pathways per trait
Biological Insights:
  • Fertility achieved through different reproductive strategies in DUK vs. DUC
  • Body mass lines use distinct metabolic and growth pathways
  • Endurance involves metabolic reprogramming and mitochondrial changes
  • Control line preserves 89% of original genetic diversity
Broader Impact: Demonstrates how sustained selection shapes genomes and reveals genetic complexity underlying extreme phenotypes.
Significance & Implications
Evolutionary Biology Insights
  • Selection vs. Drift: Demonstrates combined effects of directional selection and genetic drift
  • Genetic Architecture: Shows how polygenic traits respond to long-term selection
  • Fixation Patterns: Many beneficial alleles reached fixation alongside neutral variants
  • Bottleneck Effects: Population reduction during relocation influenced current genetic structure
Applied Breeding Implications
  • Model Validation: Confirms theoretical predictions about selection response
  • Breeding Strategies: Multiple genetic paths can achieve similar phenotypic goals
  • Diversity Management: Importance of maintaining genetic diversity in breeding programs
  • Marker Development: Identified genes provide targets for genomic selection
Biomedical Research Value
  • Disease Models: Extreme lines provide models for obesity, fertility disorders, metabolic diseases
  • Functional Genomics: Candidate genes for human health research
  • Pharmacological Targets: Identified pathways relevant for therapeutic development
  • Comparative Studies: Framework for understanding genetic basis of complex traits
Study Limitations & Future Directions
  • Missing Founder Data: No genetic material from original 1969 founders
  • Bottleneck Confounding: Relocation events complicate selection signal detection
  • No Replication: Single selection experiment (except fertility lines)
  • Functional Validation: Candidate genes need experimental confirmation
Legacy & Future Impact

This study represents a landmark achievement in quantitative genetics, providing the first comprehensive genomic characterization of the world's longest selection experiment. The Dummerstorf mouse lines serve as a unique resource for understanding:

  • Polygenic trait architecture under extreme selection pressure
  • Evolutionary consequences of sustained artificial selection
  • Genetic basis of complex biomedically relevant traits
  • Breeding methodology optimization for sustainable genetic improvement

The extreme phenotypes achieved while maintaining viable populations demonstrate the remarkable plasticity of polygenic traits and provide invaluable insights for both evolutionary biology and practical breeding applications.
