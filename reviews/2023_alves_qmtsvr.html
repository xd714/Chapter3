üéØ Problem Statement and Innovation
üî¨ The Multi-Trait Genomic Prediction Challenge

Most genomic prediction methods focus on single traits, but many traits are correlated. Multi-trait models can borrow information across traits to improve prediction accuracy, especially for traits that are difficult or expensive to measure (like carcass traits that require slaughtering animals).

Current Limitations: Existing multi-trait genomic prediction methods assume only additive genetic effects and are computationally intensive. Support Vector Regression (SVR) can capture non-linear relationships but hasn't been effectively extended to multi-trait scenarios.
Proposed Solution: Develop a (Quasi) Multitask Support Vector Regression (QMTSVR) that can:
  • Handle multiple correlated traits simultaneously
  • Capture non-linear genetic effects
  • Use genetic algorithms for efficient hyperparameter optimization
  • Avoid estimating complex covariance matrices

  • Setup: No phenotypes available for testing animals on any trait

This approach can dramatically reduce phenotyping costs while maintaining or improving selection accuracy, providing significant competitive advantages in commercial breeding programs.
