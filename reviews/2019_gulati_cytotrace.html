Introduction & Revolutionary Discovery
üéØ Breakthrough Discovery

First demonstration that the number of detectably expressed genes per cell (gene counts) serves as a universal hallmark of developmental potential across species, tissues, and platforms. This simple yet powerful metric outperformed ~19,000 molecular signatures for predicting cellular differentiation states.

Core Challenge: Existing methods for trajectory inference in single-cell RNA-seq data require prior knowledge of starting points and direction of differentiation, limiting their application to poorly understood developmental systems like human neoplasms. No universal, unsupervised method existed for determining cellular differentiation states without a priori knowledge.

Citation: Gulati, G.S., Sikandar, S.S., Wesche, D.J., et al. (2019). Single-cell transcriptional diversity is a hallmark of developmental potential. bioRxiv preprint doi: https://doi.org/10.1101/649848

Core Discovery: Gene Counts as Universal Differentiation Marker
Gene Counts Definition
Gene Counts = Number of detectably expressed genes per cell
Simple counting of genes with expression > threshold (typically > 0.1 TPM or UMI count > 0)
Why Gene Counts Work
Biological Basis:
  • Chromatin Accessibility: Less differentiated cells maintain looser chromatin structure permitting wider transcriptome sampling
  • Transcriptional Diversity: Stem cells express more genes to maintain developmental plasticity
  • Metabolic Activity: Higher RNA content correlates with increased gene expression breadth
  • Cellular Specialization: Differentiated cells restrict transcriptional programs to specific functions

CytoTRACE Development & Algorithm
1
Gene Counts Signature (GCS)
Calculate correlation between each gene's expression and gene counts per cell
Method: Pearson correlation between gene expression and gene counts
Output: Ranked list of genes correlated with transcriptional diversity
Advantage: Dataset-specific but universal principle
Result: Top-performing feature in systematic screen
2

Genes Selected: Top genes with positive correlation to gene counts
Robustness: Inherently resistant to dropout events
Interpretation: Higher values = less differentiated cells
3
Transcriptional Covariance Smoothing
Apply two-step smoothing procedure based on cell-cell similarity
Step 1: Calculate transcriptional similarity matrix between cells
Step 2: Smooth GCS values using weighted neighborhood averaging
Objective: Reduce single-cell noise while preserving biological signal
Output: Final CytoTRACE scores (0-1 scale, 1 = most stem-like)
4
Rank Transformation & Interpretation
Convert to relative ranks for consistent interpretation across datasets
Scaling: Min-max normalization to [0,1] range
Interpretation: 1.0 = most stem-like, 0.0 = most differentiated
Advantage: Comparable across different experimental systems
Application: Direct use for trajectory inference and marker discovery
