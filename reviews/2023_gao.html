Genome-wide association analysis of heifer livability and early first calving in Holstein cattle

Large-scale GWAS reveals MHC region QTL for early first calving in dairy heifers

Authors: Yahui Gao, Alexis Marceau, Victoria Iqbal, Jose Antonio Torres-Vázquez, Mahesh Neupane, Jicai Jiang, George E. Liu, Li Ma

Journal: BMC Genomics, Vol. 24, Article 628, 2023

Institution: University of Maryland, USDA-ARS, North Carolina State University

View Original Paper

Study Overview & Significance
🧬 Major Discovery

First large-scale GWAS analysis of heifer livability and early first calving in Holstein cattle, leveraging the extensive US dairy genomic database with over 3.6 million genotyped animals. Identified a major QTL in the bovine MHC region specifically associated with early first calving, revealing potential connections between immune system function and reproductive performance.

Research Challenge: Heifer health and fertility are critical for dairy farm profitability but represent complex traits with low heritability. Despite their economic importance, the genetic basis of heifer livability and early first calving remained largely unstudied due to limited data availability and the challenging nature of these traits in young, developing animals.

Citation: Gao, Y., Marceau, A., Iqbal, V., Torres-Vázquez, J.A., Neupane, M., Jiang, J., Liu, G.E., & Ma, L. (2023). Genome-wide association analysis of heifer livability and early first calving in Holstein cattle. BMC Genomics, 24, 628.

Study Context:

  • Largest GWAS study of heifer traits to date using 3.6M genotyped Holstein cattle
  • First to identify MHC region association with early first calving
  • Integrated functional genomics and transcriptome-wide association study (TWAS)
  • Utilized recently developed CDCB evaluations for heifer traits
Trait Definitions & Heritability
Heifer Livability
Definition: Expected survival percentage of an animal's female offspring from 2 days after birth up to 18 months of age under average management conditions. Key Characteristics:
  • Measures overall resistance to mortality causes
  • Primarily related to digestive and respiratory disease resistance
  • Larger, positive values are more favorable
  • Heritability: 0.0223 (very low)
Economic Impact:
  • Directly affects farm profitability through reduced losses
  • Influences replacement heifer availability
  • Reduces veterinary treatment costs
Early First Calving (EFC)
Definition: Age at first calving, representing heifer fertility and reproductive readiness. Key Characteristics:
  • Critical for optimal dairy farm productivity
  • Reduces unproductive periods
  • Enables faster generation turnover
  • Heritability: 0.0328 (very low)
Farm Benefits:
  • Increased lifetime milk production
  • Improved reproductive efficiency
  • Enhanced selection progress
  • Environmental sustainability through reduced feed costs
Experimental Design & Methods
1
Discovery Dataset
Large-scale GWAS using comprehensive US dairy database
Sample Size: 3,649,734 genotyped Holstein cattle
Composition: 336,386 bulls + 3,313,348 cows
Genotyping: Various SNP chips (3-50K) imputed to 79,060 SNPs
Final Analysis: 510,318 (livability) and 768,645 (EFC) animals after QC
2
Fine-mapping Dataset
High-resolution analysis using sequence-level variants
Sample Size: 27,235 bulls with highly accurate phenotypes
Genotyping: 50K SNP chips imputed to 3,148,506 sequence variants
Imputation Reference: 444 Holstein bulls from 1000 Bull Genomes Project
Final Analysis: 11,562 (livability) and 10,700 (EFC) bulls
3
Statistical Analysis
Advanced mixed model approach with reliability weighting
Software: SLEMM for large-scale mixed models
Phenotype: Deregressed PTA values from CDCB evaluations
Weighting: Error weight = 1/r²-1 (r² = reliability)
Significance: Bonferroni-corrected genome-wide threshold
4
Functional Analysis
Multi-layered post-GWAS analysis for biological interpretation
GO Analysis: KOBAS for pathway enrichment
Functional Enrichment: 14 chromatin states across functional regions
TWAS: S-PrediXcan with CattleGTEx v.1 eQTL models
Tissues: 24 cattle tissues for gene expression analysis

  • 118 candidate genes identified near suggestive SNPs

MHC Region - Immune-Reproduction Connection
🔬 Biological Insight

The identification of a major QTL in the bovine MHC region for early first calving provides compelling evidence for a connection between immune system function and reproductive performance. This finding aligns with the known role of the immune system in female reproduction, from egg development to pregnancy maintenance.

MHC Region Characteristics
  • Location: Bovine Taurus Autosome (BTA) 23
  • Function: Encodes molecules regulating immune response
  • Previous Associations: Cow livability and immune diseases
  • Evolutionary Importance: Highly conserved across species
  • Polygenic Nature: Multiple genes with immune functions
Immune-Reproduction Biology
  • Reproductive Process: Immune system involved throughout
  • Fetal Tolerance: Balance between protection and acceptance
  • Infection Control: Critical during pregnancy
  • Overactive Response: Can cause infertility or miscarriage
  • Underactive Response: Increases infection susceptibility

Study Limitations & Considerations
Technical Limitations
Low Heritability Challenges: Both traits have very low heritability (2.23% and 3.28%), limiting detection power for individual QTL despite large sample sizes. This reflects the complex, multifactorial nature of health and fertility traits. Phenotype Complexity: Deregressed PTA values, while standardized, may not capture all biological variation in livability and fertility. Environmental effects and management practices significantly influence trait expression. Population Specificity: Results are specific to Holstein cattle in US management systems. Generalizability to other breeds, populations, or production systems requires validation.
Methodological Strengths:
  • Large Sample Size: Unprecedented scale with 3.6M genotyped animals
  • Multiple Analysis Levels: Discovery and fine-mapping datasets
  • Functional Integration: TWAS and chromatin state enrichment
  • Quality Control: Rigorous filtering and reliability weighting
  • Statistical Rigor: Appropriate multiple testing correction
Conclusions & Scientific Significance
Key Contributions: This study represents the most comprehensive genetic analysis of heifer health and fertility traits to date. The identification of the MHC region QTL for early first calving provides novel insights into the genetic basis of reproductive performance and establishes a foundation for immune-system-focused breeding strategies. The integration of functional genomics data demonstrates the value of multi-layered approaches for complex trait analysis.
🌟 Scientific Impact

Paradigm Shift: This research establishes a new paradigm for understanding heifer fertility through immune system function, opening avenues for innovative breeding strategies that consider the immune-reproduction axis. The large-scale dataset and functional validation approaches set new standards for livestock genomics research.
