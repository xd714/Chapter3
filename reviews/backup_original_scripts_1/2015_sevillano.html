Genome-wide Association Study using Deregressed Breeding Values

GWAS for Cryptorchidism and Scrotal/Inguinal Hernia in Pigs

Authors: Claudia A. Sevillano, Marcos S. Lopes, Barbara Harlizius, Egiel H.A.T. Hanenberg, Egbert F. Knol, John W.M. Bastiaansen

Institution: Topigs Norsvin & Wageningen University

Journal: Genetics Selection Evolution (2015) 47:18

View Original Paper

Paper Information & Citation

Citation: Sevillano, C.A., Lopes, M.S., Harlizius, B., Hanenberg, E.H.A.T., Knol, E.F., & Bastiaansen, J.W.M. (2015). Genome-wide association study using deregressed breeding values for cryptorchidism and scrotal/inguinal hernia in two pig lines. Genetics Selection Evolution, 47, 18.

Key Innovation: This study demonstrates a novel approach for GWAS of rare congenital defects by using deregressed estimated breeding values (DEBV) instead of direct phenotypes, enabling analysis when affected animals are typically culled and not available for genotyping.

Study Design and Methodology
1
Phenotype Collection
Large-scale phenotype recording across commercial breeding programs
Records: 1,360,453 purebred and crossbred male offspring
Populations: Large White (LW) and Landrace (LR) lines
Farms: 57 different commercial farms
Traits: Binary recording (0/1) for cryptorchidism and hernia
2
Genotyping Strategy
Strategic genotyping of genetically related animals to maximize information
LW animals: 2,570 genotyped (608 males, 1,962 females)
LR animals: 2,272 genotyped (835 males, 1,437 females)
Platform: Porcine SNP60 BeadChip (Illumina)
Strategy: Genotype parents/ancestors, not affected offspring
3
Quality Control Pipeline
Rigorous two-step quality control for genotype data
SNP filters: GenCall score ≥0.15, call rate ≥0.95, MAF ≥0.01
Animal filters: Missing genotype frequency ≤0.05
Final datasets: LW: 2,102 animals, 38,632 SNPs; LR: 1,926 animals, 39,508 SNPs
Call rate: 99.6% for both populations
4
Breeding Value Estimation
Binary trait animal model for EBV estimation using comprehensive pedigree
Model: Yijklmn = μ + HYSi + TNBj + Pk + LLl + c²m + an + eijklmn
Pedigree: 1,434,713 animals up to 20 generations
Software: ASReml with REML methodology
Fixed effects: Herd-year-season, litter size, parity, litter type
Novel Methodological Approach
Deregressed Breeding Values (DEBV) Methodology

The key innovation is using DEBV as response variables in GWAS instead of direct phenotypes. This approach enables genetic analysis of rare defects where affected animals are typically not available for genotyping, connecting genotype data from healthy relatives to phenotype information from affected offspring.

Deregression Process
Method: Garrick et al. (2009) approach
DEBV = EBV - parental average effects
Purpose:
• Remove parental contributions
• Obtain individual genetic merit
• Create pseudo-phenotypes for GWAS

Weighting: Based on reliability with scalar c = 0.5
GWAS Model
Association Model:
DEBVij √w = μ + SNPi + aj + eij
Components:
• DEBVij = deregressed breeding value
• w = weighting factor based on reliability
• SNPi = genotype effect (0, 1, 2)
• aj = additive genetic effect

Significance: FDR ≤ 0.20 for multiple testing correction
Key Findings and Results
Variance Components
Heritability Estimates:
• Cryptorchidism: 0.26 ± 0.02
• Scrotal/inguinal hernia: 0.31 ± 0.01

Common Litter Effects:
• Cryptorchidism: 0.64
• Hernia: 0.53

Incidence Rates:
• Cryptorchidism: 0.22% overall
• Hernia: 0.39% overall
QTL Discovery
Cryptorchidism:
• LW: 7 QTL regions (22 significant SNPs)
• LR: 31 QTL regions (83 significant SNPs)
• Overlap: SSC8 (65-73 Mb)

Hernia:
• LW: 5 QTL regions (10 significant SNPs)
• LR: 5 QTL regions (22 significant SNPs)
• Overlap: SSC13 (34-37 Mb)

Variance Explained: 0.48-2.77% per SNP
Candidate Genes and Biological Insights
Cryptorchidism Candidates
SSC8 Region (Both populations):
  • GNRHR: Gonadotropin-releasing hormone receptor - mutations cause undescended testes in mice
  • AFP: Alpha-fetoprotein - interacts with estrogens, may affect testicular descent
  • PDGFRA & v-KIT: Associated with male gonad development and cryptorchidism in humans
SSC13 Region (LW):
  • GATA2: Critical role in urogenital development, regulated by FOG2
Hernia Candidates
SSC13 Region (Both populations):
  • RHOA: Regulates smooth muscle contraction and processus vaginalis obliteration
SSC8 Region (LW):
  • EGF: Mutations related to connective tissue problems and inguinal hernia
  • LEF1: Associates with β-catenin, involved in gubernaculum swelling during testicular descent
Biological Pathway: Incomplete obliteration of processus vaginalis leads to both cryptorchidism and hernia
Methodological Impact
Novel Contributions:
  • First successful GWAS for pig congenital defects using DEBV approach
  • Demonstrates power of connecting genotypes from healthy animals to phenotypes from affected relatives
  • Identified several novel QTL regions and narrowed confidence intervals for known regions
Breeding Applications:
  • SNPs can contribute to genomic selection indices
  • Enables genetic improvement for rare traits
  • Animal welfare and economic benefits
Study Limitations and Future Directions
Challenges and Limitations
Small Effect Sizes: Individual SNPs explain only 0.48-2.77% of variance, indicating highly polygenic architecture typical of complex traits. Population-Specific Effects: Limited overlap between LW and LR populations suggests population-specific genetic architectures or different linkage patterns. Causative Variants: SNP associations may reflect linkage disequilibrium rather than direct causative effects, requiring fine-mapping and functional validation.
Future Research Directions
Genomic Applications: Implementation of genomic estimated breeding values (GEBV) rather than individual SNPs for practical breeding programs. Functional Studies: Validation of candidate genes through expression studies and functional genomics approaches. Cross-Species Validation: Investigation of homologous regions in other livestock species and model organisms. Fine-Mapping: Higher density genotyping or whole-genome sequencing to identify causative variants within QTL regions.
Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Problem Statement
Challenge: Genetic analysis of rare congenital defects is difficult because affected animals are typically culled and not available for genotyping.
  • Cryptorchidism and hernia are important welfare and economic concerns
  • Traditional GWAS approaches require genotyping affected individuals
  • Previous studies had limited power due to small sample sizes
  • Need for novel methodological approaches
Opportunity: Use breeding value methodology to connect genotype data from healthy relatives to phenotype data from affected offspring.
HOW - Innovative Methodology
DEBV Approach: Novel application of deregressed estimated breeding values as pseudo-phenotypes in GWAS.
  • Step 1: Estimate breeding values using comprehensive pedigree and phenotype data
  • Step 2: Deregress breeding values to remove parental effects
  • Step 3: Use DEBV as response variable in single-SNP GWAS
  • Validation: Apply across two pig populations with rigorous quality control
Statistical Innovation: Proper weighting based on reliability and FDR correction for multiple testing.
WHAT - Key Achievements
Methodological Success: Demonstrated that DEBV approach enables powerful GWAS for rare traits.
  • Novel QTL regions identified for both traits
  • Candidate genes with strong biological relevance
  • Cross-population validation for key regions
  • Polygenic architecture revealed for both defects
Practical Impact: Provides foundation for genomic selection against congenital defects in pig breeding programs, with applications to animal welfare and economic efficiency.
